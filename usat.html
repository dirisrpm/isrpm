A structured self-assessment against ubiquitous technical surveillance. Work through each practice; your end-user-device signature is scored across the UTS collection vectors, with prioritized actions to reduce it.

This assessment turns disciplined cyber-hygiene practices into a read on your personal digital signature — how observable, identifiable, and correlatable you are to an adversary running ubiquitous technical surveillance (UTS). You'll answer 48 questions across seven practical areas. There are no right answers to perform for; an honest self-check is the only thing that produces a useful result. The practices here are compiled from publicly available sources; treat your completed assessment as potentially sensitive in the aggregate.

Nothing here is saved, sent, or tracked. This tool runs entirely in your browser — no cookies, no local storage, and no third-party calls. Your answers live only in memory for this session, are never transmitted to or stored on any server, and disappear when you close the tab. The only output is the report you choose to generate.
